# Strategic Reshoring of Critical Mineral and Energy Supply Chains

### Theme

The era of frictionless global trade is yielding to a transactional geopolitical order where resource sovereignty, secure maritime corridors, and state-led industrial policy define the new investment frontier. Capital is transitioning from a regime of cost-optimization and "Just-in-Time" logistics to one of "Just-in-Case" resilience, driven by a deteriorating global security environment and the weaponization of critical mineral chokepoints. This structural shift prioritizes the creation of independent, Western-allied supply chains for liquefied natural gas and strategic minerals, presenting a generational opportunity for institutional capital to rotate into the "Reshoring Basket" of miners, midstream processors, and energy exporters protected by sovereign price floors and strategic reserves.

### Executive Summary

As the global economy enters 2026, the long-standing architecture of neoliberal globalization is being fundamentally dismantled by a confluence of systemic shocks and strategic pivots. Global economic output is settling into a persistently slower growth path, forecast at 2.7 per cent for 2026, as the world navigates an era defined by trade realignments and geostrategic fragmentation. The deteriorating security environment—exemplified by a 90% collapse in commercial tanker traffic through the Strait of Hormuz following the escalation of the US-Israel conflict with Iran—has decapitated traditional energy flows, sending Brent crude prices up 39% and forcing a total reconfiguration of the liquefied natural gas (LNG) market. Simultaneously, China has escalated its "chokehold" strategy by imposing aggressive export controls on rare earth elements (REEs), graphite, and lithium-battery precursors, nearly halting global high-tech and defense manufacturing. In response, Western governments have abandoned non-interventionist dogmas in favor of muscular industrial policies. The United States’ launch of "Project Vault"—a $12 billion strategic mineral reserve—and the establishment of the Forum on Resource Geostrategic Engagement (FORGE) mark the end of the market-clearing price era for strategic inputs. Institutional investors now face a bifurcated market: a "Democratic Security Diamond" involving the US, Australia, Canada, and Japan is creating a ring-fenced ecosystem of "safe" supply. This report identifies a shift in value toward vertically integrated "Mine-to-Magnet" operators, U.S. LNG exporters with massive distributable cash flow (DCF) potential, and specialized midstream processors that benefit from government-guaranteed price floors and debt financing.
